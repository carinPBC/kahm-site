Outreach Activity

Total Full-Time Employees as of May 2026: 7

In the year stated above, Prescott Broadcasting, LLC performed several outreach activities.

The General Sales Manager and Vice-President of Prescott Broadcasting, LLC, Janice Derks, continued her long-time program of training the sales personnel in order to both heighten their skills as salespeople as well as preparing them for potential promotion within the sales organization to Sales Manager and possibly beyond. The training program consisted of all salespeople meeting with Mrs. Derks at least a few times a week, if not every day for at least 15–30 minutes or more if necessary. Each meeting consisted of critiques of sales calls, along with role playing exercises and other training for the salespeople.

The General Manager and President of Prescott Broadcasting, LLC, Jason Zinzilieta began and continues the training process for one new person in order to heighten their skills in the production and programming departments in order to eventually move them into the positions of Production Director and Program Director and possibly in the future as General Manager. The training consists of meetings with Zinzilieta at least a few times a week for at least 15–30 minutes which includes critiques of production and programming decisions and assignments to be completed.

The station is currently employing a local college student as a paid intern in the hopes of furthering the student’s education in journalism as it relates to radio station operations. The student has worked part-time during the 2025–26 school year averaging 12 hours per week, and will continue to work in our internship program during the summer 2026 to continue his radio education as he continues his college education locally.

The station is also currently employing two local college students as paid interns in the hopes of furthering the student’s education in journalism as it relates to meteorology in broadcasting. The two students are meteorology students at a local university and are using their knowledge to predict and report the local weather forecast on weekdays, one student 3 days a week and the other student 2 days a week. Their employment began December 15, 2025 and continues in 2026 as they continue their own university education locally.

General Manager and President of Prescott Broadcasting, LLC, Mr. Jason Zinzilieta attended two job fairs during the time stated above and conducted job recruitment and interviews, along with community education about jobs and careers in the broadcasting business and radio’s role in the community. These job fairs were: Prescott Valley Job Fair – September 10, 2025 at the Findlay Toyota Center in Prescott Valley, and the Prescott Job Fair – April 8, 2026 at Yavapai College. Both job fairs were hosted by Yavapai College.
